Your garage door is the largest moving part of your home. It opens and closes over 1,500 times a year for the average family — and every cycle puts wear on springs, cables, rollers, tracks, and the opener motor. Just like your car needs oil changes to avoid engine failure, your garage door needs regular tune-ups to avoid expensive breakdowns.

### What a Professional Tune-Up Includes

At Garage Goat, our preventative maintenance tune-up covers every component of your garage door system:

- **Lubrication:** We apply professional-grade silicone lubricant to all moving parts — springs, rollers, hinges, bearing plates, and the opener chain or screw. Proper lubrication reduces friction, quiets operation, and prevents rust (a major issue in Houston's humidity).
- **Hardware tightening:** Vibration from daily use loosens bolts, brackets, and track mounts over time. We check and tighten every piece of hardware to factory specifications.
- **Balance check:** We disconnect the opener and test the door's manual balance. An unbalanced door strains the opener and accelerates spring wear.
- **Safety sensor test:** We verify that the auto-reverse sensors are properly aligned and responding correctly. These sensors prevent the door from closing on people, pets, and objects.
- **Track inspection and alignment:** We check tracks for bends, dents, and debris, and verify proper alignment on both sides.
- **Spring inspection:** We visually inspect springs for rust, gaps in the coils, and signs of wear. We also test tension to ensure proper balance.
- **Weatherstripping check:** We inspect the bottom seal and side seals for cracks, gaps, and deterioration. Good weatherstripping keeps out pests, moisture, and extreme temperatures.
- **Opener performance test:** We test the opener's force settings, travel limits, and safety reverse to make sure everything operates within manufacturer specifications.

### How Often Should You Schedule a Tune-Up?

We recommend a professional tune-up **once a year** for most homeowners. If you use your garage door more than 4 times per day, or if your door is over 10 years old, twice a year is ideal. The best times to schedule are **spring** (before the brutal Texas summer) and **fall** (before holiday season when you're opening and closing more frequently).

### Seasonal Tips for Texas Heat and Humidity

Cypress and NW Houston homeowners face unique challenges that make regular maintenance even more important:

- **Summer heat:** Metal components expand in extreme heat, which can throw off track alignment and opener travel limits. Lubricant also breaks down faster in 100°F+ temperatures.
- **Humidity:** Houston's humidity causes rust and corrosion on springs, hinges, and tracks. Regular lubrication creates a protective barrier against moisture.
- **Storm season:** After any significant storm, inspect your door for wind damage, debris in the tracks, and water intrusion at the bottom seal.

### The Cost of Skipping Maintenance

A tune-up costs a fraction of the repairs it prevents. Consider: a spring replacement runs $200–$400, an opener replacement costs $400–$600, and track repair can hit $150–$300. One annual tune-up can prevent all three by catching problems before they become failures.
