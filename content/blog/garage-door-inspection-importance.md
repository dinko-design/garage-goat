Your garage door is one of the hardest-working systems in your home, cycling up and down over 1,500 times per year. Despite that constant use, most homeowners never think about inspecting it until something breaks. Regular professional inspections are the most effective way to prevent costly repairs, extend your door's lifespan, and — most importantly — keep your family safe.

### Why Inspections Matter More Than You Think

According to the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC), garage doors cause an estimated **20,000–30,000 injuries per year** in the United States. Many of these injuries involve doors that fell due to worn springs or cables, malfunctioning auto-reverse safety features, or children caught under a closing door with failed sensors. A professional inspection specifically targets these failure points before they become hazards.

### What Professionals Check That Homeowners Miss

Homeowners can spot obvious issues — a loud noise, a door that sticks. But trained technicians look deeper:

- **Spring tension and cycle wear:** We can estimate how many cycles your springs have left and whether they're losing tension. Most homeowners don't notice gradual tension loss until the spring snaps.
- **Cable fraying:** Cables are under extreme tension and often fray from the inside out. By the time you see visible damage, the cable may be at 50% strength.
- **Roller bearing condition:** Worn roller bearings create noise and friction, but they also indicate that the roller could seize — causing the door to jam or jump off the track.
- **Track alignment:** Even a 1/4-inch misalignment can cause premature wear on rollers and put lateral stress on the door panels. We use levels and measuring tools to verify alignment.
- **Opener force calibration:** Over time, opener force settings can drift. If the opener applies too much force, it can damage the door. Too little force, and it can't fully open or close. We test and calibrate to manufacturer specifications.
- **Auto-reverse function:** The auto-reverse is your door's most critical safety feature. We test both the mechanical force reverse and the photo-eye sensors to ensure they're working correctly.
- **Weatherstripping integrity:** Deteriorated seals let in moisture, pests, and extreme temperatures — all of which accelerate wear on your garage door system and affect your energy bills.

### How Often Should You Get an Inspection?

For most homeowners in the Cypress and NW Houston area, we recommend a **professional inspection once a year**. However, you should schedule a semi-annual inspection if:

- Your door is more than 10 years old
- You use your garage door 4+ times per day
- You've noticed any unusual noises, movements, or delays
- Your home recently experienced a significant storm or temperature swing
