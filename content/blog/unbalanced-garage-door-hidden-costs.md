Most homeowners don't think about garage door balance until something breaks. But an unbalanced door is one of the most common — and most expensive — problems we see at Garage Goat. It doesn't announce itself with a loud bang like a broken spring. Instead, it quietly strains every component of your system, day after day, until something gives out.

### What Does "Unbalanced" Actually Mean?

Your garage door is balanced by a system of torsion springs (or extension springs on older doors) that counterbalance the door's weight. When properly balanced, the springs hold roughly half the door's weight, and the opener handles the rest with minimal effort. When the balance is off — even slightly — the entire system compensates, and that compensation costs you money.

### The Hidden Costs of an Unbalanced Door

- **Opener motor burnout:** Your opener is designed to lift a balanced door, not carry the full weight. An unbalanced door forces the motor to work 2–3x harder on every cycle, dramatically shortening its lifespan. A $400–$600 opener replacement that could have been avoided.
- **Accelerated spring wear:** When one spring bears more load than the other, it cycles through its lifespan faster. Instead of lasting 10–15 years, your springs may fail in 5–7 years.
- **Higher energy bills:** An unbalanced door often leaves gaps in the weatherstripping or doesn't seal flush against the frame. In Texas summers, that means conditioned air escaping and hot air rushing in — especially if your garage is attached to your home.
- **Safety risks:** An unbalanced door can fall unexpectedly if the opener fails or power goes out. A door that weighs 150–250 lbs dropping uncontrolled is a serious injury hazard for your family, pets, and vehicles.
- **Track and roller damage:** Uneven weight distribution causes the door to track unevenly, wearing out rollers on one side faster and potentially bending the tracks over time.

### How to Test Your Garage Door Balance

This simple test takes 60 seconds and can save you hundreds in future repairs:

- **Step 1:** Close the garage door completely.
- **Step 2:** Pull the emergency release cord to disconnect the opener.
- **Step 3:** Manually lift the door to the halfway point (about waist height).
- **Step 4:** Let go carefully. A properly balanced door should stay in place — hovering at the halfway mark with minimal movement.

If the door slides down, the springs are too weak or stretched. If it shoots up, the springs have too much tension. Either way, it's out of balance and needs professional adjustment.

### Why Professional Adjustment Matters

**Never attempt to adjust garage door springs yourself.** Torsion springs are under extreme tension — hundreds of pounds of stored energy — and improper handling can cause serious injury or death. This is not a DIY job.

A professional balance adjustment involves precisely calibrating spring tension, checking cable condition and drum alignment, inspecting rollers and tracks for wear, and verifying that the opener force settings are correct.
